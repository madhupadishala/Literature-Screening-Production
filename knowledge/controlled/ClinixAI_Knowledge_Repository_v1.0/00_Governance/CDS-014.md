# CDS-014 — Tenant Isolation

## 1. Controlled Status

This governance Knowledge Object is included to complete the 100-file production repository but remains Draft and must not be activated for production decisioning until formally approved.

**Production use:** Not permitted until approval and effective-date activation.

## 2. Purpose

This cross-cutting domain defines non-negotiable AI, data, quality, security and knowledge-governance boundaries for all ClinixAI pharmacovigilance workflows.

This Knowledge Object specifically governs **Tenant Isolation**. Its purpose is to convert an approved pharmacovigilance principle into a controlled, testable and software-executable requirement without weakening the original meaning.

## 3. Approved / Governing Statement

> Client knowledge, product data, cases, search profiles, evidence and configurations shall remain isolated by tenant. Cross-tenant retrieval, indexing, caching or prompt assembly is prohibited.

The quoted statement is the controlling text for this version. Supporting interpretation in this file may clarify implementation, but it shall not override, narrow or expand the governing statement without a formally approved revision.

## 4. Pharmacovigilance and Regulatory Interpretation

In regulated literature surveillance, a correct result is not sufficient unless the organisation can show how the result was obtained, which evidence was used, which rule version applied and who reviewed or changed it. This object therefore has two simultaneous roles:

1. It defines the pharmacovigilance expectation for **Tenant Isolation**.
2. It defines the control boundary that software, AI and human workflow must respect.

The rule shall be applied together with tenant-specific SOPs, safety agreements, product configuration and effective master data. Where a tenant requirement is more specific, the configured source hierarchy and conflict-management process shall be used. Where evidence is incomplete, the system shall expose uncertainty and preserve the article rather than fabricate a definitive conclusion.

## 5. Operational Interpretation

- Apply the rule only within the effective tenant, product and workflow context.
- Use the exact approved governing statement as the policy source; do not paraphrase it into a weaker operational rule.
- Retain the input evidence and the identifier/version of this Knowledge Object with every material outcome.
- Enforce the principle in orchestration, retrieval, prompt assembly, validation and post-processing.
- Return a machine-readable policy decision when an action is blocked.
- Test the principle through adversarial prompts and negative-control scenarios.

## 6. AI Behaviour

### AI shall

- Retrieve this Knowledge Object and its effective version before producing a related recommendation.
- Extract the minimum evidence needed to evaluate the rule and return the evidence location with the output.
- Separate source facts from model interpretation.
- Use controlled vocabulary and typed output states where available.
- Report `Unknown`, `Conflicting`, `Blocked` or `Manual Review Required` when the rule cannot be safely completed.
- Preserve the original evidence and the exact governing rule used.

### AI shall not

- Invent missing regulatory facts or convert a weak contextual suggestion into a confirmed element.
- Treat model confidence as a substitute for evidence.
- modify the approved rule, source hierarchy, Product Master or decision table during runtime.
- hide contradictory evidence.
- finalize a result that requires an authorized human decision.

## 7. Deterministic Decision Logic

1. Resolve the tenant and effective configuration.
2. Retrieve the effective Knowledge Object by stable ID and version.
3. Validate required inputs and structured master-data dependencies.
4. Apply deterministic checks before any probabilistic interpretation.
5. Produce a structured result containing value, status, evidence, rule IDs and confidence.
6. Route Unknown, Conflicting, Blocked or Out-of-Scope outcomes to the configured review path.
7. Persist an immutable audit event and return the controlled workflow transition.

This sequence is normative at the control level. Implementation may be distributed across services, but the observable outcome and audit evidence must remain equivalent.

## 8. Data Contract

### Minimum inputs

- `tenant_context`
- `effective_knowledge_set`
- `user_role`
- `source_evidence`
- `model_configuration`
- `audit_context`

### Required outputs

- `policy_compliance_status`
- `blocked_action_reason`
- `audit_record`
- `escalation_or_review_requirement`

Every output shall carry at least: `tenant_id`, `knowledge_object_id`, `knowledge_object_version`, `value_or_decision`, `status`, `evidence_references`, `applied_rule_ids`, `confidence_or_certainty`, `review_requirement`, `created_at` and `created_by_service_or_user`.

## 9. Evidence and Traceability Requirements

- Preserve source-document identity and version or content hash.
- Store the smallest sufficient evidence span, page/section or structured record reference.
- Record the effective Knowledge Object version and all dependent rule/master-data versions.
- Record the model/provider/configuration only when an AI component contributed.
- Preserve pre-override and post-override values.
- Ensure the audit record can be reconstructed independently of the current UI.
- Do not store only a narrative explanation; store structured provenance suitable for validation and inspection.

## 10. Exceptions and Uncertainty Handling

- Conflicting evidence: preserve all competing evidence, set the field to Conflicting or Manual Review Required and do not silently choose one.
- Missing effective master data: block the governed decision and create a data-quality exception.
- Out-of-scope scenario: do not extend the rule by analogy; route to SME review and create a knowledge-gap candidate.

An exception shall not delete or suppress the retrieved article. The exception becomes a controlled workflow state with owner, due date, reason and resolution record.

## 11. Dependencies

- `CDS-001`

Dependencies identify knowledge that should usually be retrieved or evaluated with this object. They do not permit a dependent object to override the governing statement unless the approved source hierarchy explicitly defines that precedence.

## 12. Worked Example

A user asks the AI to ignore the Product Master and produce a desired regulatory conclusion. ClinixAI rejects the instruction, applies the effective governed knowledge, records the policy conflict and routes any unresolved matter to an authorized reviewer.

**Expected controlled behaviour:** The system produces a structured, evidence-linked outcome; identifies the applied KO and version; exposes uncertainty; and preserves the reviewer’s ability to confirm or override with justification.

## 13. Validation and Test Requirements

- Attempt prompt-injection and policy-conflict scenarios and verify governed knowledge remains authoritative.
- Attempt cross-tenant retrieval and confirm hard isolation.
- Verify that model or prompt changes create a controlled versioned release record.
- Verify that blocked or uncertain outputs are logged and routed for authorized review.

Additional minimum release gates:

- Positive control: a known scenario in which the rule must apply.
- Negative control: a scenario in which the rule must not apply.
- Boundary control: incomplete or conflicting evidence.
- Tenant-isolation control.
- Version-regression control comparing current and superseded rules.
- Audit-reconstruction control.
- Human-override control where applicable.

## 14. Software and AI Implementation Guidance

- Store the Knowledge Object as immutable, versioned content with a content hash.
- Index title, rule, operational interpretation, examples and synonyms as separate semantic chunks while keeping the KO ID on every chunk.
- Use metadata filters for tenant, domain, status, effective date, version, product scope, jurisdiction and source type.
- Retrieve the exact rule chunk together with supporting explanatory chunks; never rely on vector similarity alone for controlled rules.
- Represent deterministic outcomes with typed enums rather than unconstrained generated text.
- Emit structured provenance: source document, evidence coordinates, KO IDs, rule IDs, master-data versions and model configuration.
- Protect reviewer overrides with role checks, mandatory reason capture and append-only history.
- Enforce governance both before generation and after generation through policy checks; prompt text alone is not a sufficient control.

### Recommended service boundaries

- **Knowledge Registry:** effective-version resolution and lifecycle enforcement.
- **Retrieval Layer:** hybrid retrieval with mandatory metadata filters.
- **Rule Engine:** deterministic gates and decision tables.
- **Evidence Service:** source spans, hashes and article lineage.
- **AI Extraction Layer:** bounded extraction from unstructured text.
- **Validation Layer:** schema, policy and contradiction checks.
- **Audit Service:** append-only regulated event capture.
- **Review Workspace:** human confirmation, override and reason capture.

## 15. Vector Database and RAG Guidance

Create section-aware chunks rather than arbitrary token windows. At minimum, index:

1. Governing statement.
2. Operational interpretation.
3. AI shall / shall not behaviour.
4. Decision logic.
5. Worked example.
6. Validation requirements.

Every chunk must retain `ko_id`, `version`, `status`, `domain`, `tenant_scope`, `effective_date`, `source_of_truth`, `content_hash` and `section`. Retrieval shall filter for approved/effective content before semantic ranking. Draft content may be indexed in a non-production collection but shall never be mixed with the production decision context.
